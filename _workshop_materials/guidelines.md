# Beginning of utterances
## Canonincal Placement of initial segments
Consider the canonical placements below when determing whether a boundary is placed "correctly" or not. 
- If they follow these canonical placements, select 'Y' on the script. 
- If they do not follow the canonical placements, select 'N' on the script and move the boundary to a more canonical location.

### Stop Consonants and Affricates
Phones: P, T, K, B, D, G, CH, and JH

#### Voiceless stop consonants
In English, voiceless stops have a longer period of noise following release of the burst than voiced consonant do. The boundary should be placed adjacent to and preceding the release of the burst.

Example: ![voiceless stop](img/initial_vlstop.jpg)

#### Voiced stop consonants
For voiced stops without pre-voicing, the boundary should be placed adjacent to the short release burst. This may result in a very short segmental duration between the stop burst and the onset of the following vowel.

Example: ![voiceless stop](img/initial_vdstop.jpg)

For voiced stops with pre-voicing, the onset boundary of the segment should include any pre-voicing; place preceding and adjacent to the voicing onset.

Example: ![voiceless stop](img/initial_vdstop_pv.jpg)

#### Fricatives
Phones: S, Z, H, SH, ZH, F, V, TH, and DH

The boundary should be placed adjacently preceding the first sign of frication noise for the fricative.

Example: ![voiceless stop](img/initial_fricative.jpg)

#### Nasals 
Phones: M, N, and NG

The boundary is placed adjacently preceding the onset of phonation/nasalization.

Example: ![voiceless stop](img/initial_nasal.jpg)


### Liquids and rhotics
Phones: L and R

**L - Lateral Approximant**

The boundary should be placed at the onset of the segment. This may be clear phonation and intensity seen as nearly black on the spectrogram, or in the case of Figure 15, some formants started to be present as part of the articulation of L, so the boundary was placed at the onset of this noise and formant structure.

Example: ![voiceless stop](img/initial_liquid.jpg)

**R - Rhotic**
Place boundary at the onset of articulation-related noise in the signal. In this case, some articulation of R preceded phonation.

Example: ![voiceless stop](img/initial_rhotic.jpg)

### Initial vowels and glides
**Vowels**
The boundary should be placed at the onset of phonation/laryngeal activity related to the beginning of the vowel.

Example: ![voiceless stop](img/initial_vowel.jpg)

“Glottal pop” at the beginning of a vowel should be included as part of the vowel and the boundary should be placed adjacent to the glottal pulse.

**Glides (W and Y)**
The boundary placed at the onset of phonation or articulation (onset of glide may be something like a voiceless vowel).

Example: ![voiceless stop](img/initial_glide.jpg)

# Transitions Preceding Silence
## Final-consonant to silence transition
The boundary should be placed at the release of the consonant, include any phonation, aspiration, or noise associated with the release, but exclude exhalation.

### Unreleased consonants
**If no visual or auditory indication of the consonant, delete the segment.**

#### Following a vowel
*With visible glottalization and no creaky vowel, segment the glottalization*

*With visible glottalization and creaky vowel, segment the final two glottal pulses*

*Without visible glottalization but visible voicing following formant cessation, include only the voicing following formant cessation*

Place the boundary for the beginning of the unreleased consonant at the end of formant structure of the vowel, and place the final boundary at the end of visible energy related to the consonant.

#### Cluster reduction
**If no visual or auditory indication of the consonant, delete the segment.**

## Final fricative to silence transition
The boundary should be placed at the end of the noise associated with articulation of the fricative (be sure to listen to make sure that the noise doesn’t include exhalation).

## Nasals to silence transition
The boundary should placed at the end of phonation and articulation associated with the nasal consonant but excluding exhalation. [when f1 drops out]
